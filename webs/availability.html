  • Turn on Private Relay

In most of the countries, connectivity to iCloud Private Relay won't be a problem considering it's using http/2 as a fallback.

However, according to Apple, regulatory reasons prevent the company from launching Private Relay in China, Belarus, Colombia, Egypt, Kazakhstan, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, Turkmenistan, Uganda, and the Philippines.

The easiest way to enable it is to route Apple's iCloud API IP addresses through proxy, while the actural iCPR won't go through proxy. By doing this Apple actually gives a IP address inside these countries, which can be found at https://mask-api.icloud.com/egress-ip-ranges.csv, while the outbound data won't be censored as it goes through the CDN provider's internal network.
